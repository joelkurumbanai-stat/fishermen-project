	\textbf{\Large Abstract}
\end{center} 
{\doublespacing
\qquad 

This study examines the socio-economic conditions of 114 male fishermen in and around Colachel Fishing Harbor in Kanyakumari district, Tamil Nadu. The study focuses on demographic profile, income variation, expenditure pattern, fishing safety perceptions, and opinions on government welfare measures.
The findings reveal that most fishermen have low educational attainment and long years of fishing experience. Income is found to vary based on factors such as fishing experience and occupational role. Loan repayment constitutes the major share of household expenditure, indicating significant financial burden. The study also shows that there is no notable difference in safety perceptions across different fishing roles. However, a majority of respondents expressed dissatisfaction with government welfare measures related to deep-sea safety. Overall, the study highlights the economic vulnerability of the fishing community and emphasizes the need for effective policy implementation and improved support systems.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} Socio-Economic Status, Colachel Fishing Harbor, ANOVA, Regression, Expenditure Pattern, Government Welfare
